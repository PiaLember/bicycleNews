Brand History:

Pashley Cycles - hand-built in England

18 OKTOOBER 2023

Nestled in the heart of England, Pashley Cycles boasts a remarkable heritage as the nation's longest-established manufacturer of hand-built bicycles. Their story, spanning over nine decades, is woven with a passion for quality, tradition, and creating timeless two-wheeled companions.

From Humble Beginnings to Enduring Legacy (1926-Present)

Founded in 1926 in the picturesque town of Stratford-upon-Avon, Pashley Cycles began as a modest frame-building workshop. The founders, William and William (yes, you read that right!) Pashley, envisioned crafting bicycles that were not only functional but also embodied elegance and durability. Their commitment to using high-quality steel and traditional brazing techniques – a method of joining metal tubes with solder – laid the foundation for Pashley's enduring legacy.

The Allure of Hand-Built Craftsmanship

Pashley Cycles proudly stands apart from the mass-produced bicycle market. Each Pashley creation is meticulously hand-built by skilled craftspeople. This dedication to detail results in bicycles that are not just machines, but works of art. The signature lugged steel frames, built with time-tested brazing techniques, offer a strength and resilience unmatched by modern assembly line methods.

A Bicycle for Every Journey

Pashley's dedication to quality and craftsmanship extends to a diverse range of bicycles. From classic upright cycles reminiscent of a bygone era to practical cargo bikes and adventurous touring bicycles, Pashley caters to a variety of riders and cycling styles.

The Future of Pashley Cycles

As Pashley Cycles embarks on its next chapter, one thing remains constant: their unwavering commitment to heritage and quality. By blending traditional methods with modern advancements, they ensure their place in the ever-evolving cycling world. Owning a Pashley cycle is more than just acquiring a mode of transportation; it's an investment in a piece of cycling history, a testament to British craftsmanship, and a gateway to countless adventures on two wheels.
